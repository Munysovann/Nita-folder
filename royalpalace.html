Introduction to the Royal Palace

The Royal Palace of Cambodia, located in Phnom Penh, is one of the most significant and stunning landmarks in the country. Built in the 1860s by King Norodom, it serves as the official residence of the King of Cambodia. The palace complex reflects the splendor of Khmer architecture and has become a symbol of Cambodia's royal history.

The Royal Palace is a blend of traditional Khmer architecture with French colonial influences. It consists of several buildings, including the main palace where the King resides, the Throne Hall, and the Silver Pagoda. The Silver Pagoda is famous for its floor made of 5,000 silver tiles, as well as its collection of priceless treasures, including a golden Buddha statue adorned with diamonds.

Visitors to the Royal Palace can explore its expansive grounds, which are beautifully landscaped with lush gardens and impressive courtyards. The Royal Palace also plays an important role in the Cambodian monarchy, hosting official ceremonies and state functions. While some areas are open to the public, the private residences of the King remain off-limits. The Royal Palace is a major tourist attraction in Phnom Penh, offering visitors a glimpse into Cambodia's royal traditions and history.
